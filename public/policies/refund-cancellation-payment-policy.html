REFUND, CANCELLATION & PAYMENT POLICY

Applicable to:

SPEAXA Website

SPEAXA Android Application

SPEAXA iOS Application

Learning Management System (LMS)

Parent Portal

Student Portal

Teacher Portal

1. PURPOSE

This Refund, Cancellation & Payment Policy governs:

Course fee payments.

Subscription payments.

Live class payments.

Recorded course purchases.

Membership plans.

Promotional offers.

Scholarship programmes.

Refund requests.

Cancellation requests.

By making any payment to SPEAXA, the User agrees to this Policy.

2. GENERAL POLICY

SPEAXA invests substantial resources in:

Student counselling.

Parent counselling.

Teacher allocation.

Batch planning.

Academic scheduling.

Technology infrastructure.

Platform maintenance.

Teacher onboarding.

Content development.

Learning Management System (LMS).

Customer support.

Student success services.

Accordingly:

Every payment made to SPEAXA is treated as a commitment towards the selected educational programme.

Users are encouraged to review programme details carefully before making any payment.

3. PAYMENT TERMS

Users agree that:

All fees shall be paid through authorised payment channels.

Fees shall be displayed before payment confirmation.

Applicable taxes shall be charged wherever required.

Payment confirmation shall be generated electronically.

SPEAXA reserves the right to revise prices for future enrolments.

Price revisions shall not affect programmes already purchased unless expressly stated.

4. NON-REFUNDABLE POLICY

Unless otherwise required by applicable law:

Course fees are generally non-refundable.

Subscription fees are generally non-refundable.

Registration fees are generally non-refundable.

Admission fees are generally non-refundable.

Convenience fees are generally non-refundable.

Technology fees are generally non-refundable.

Processing charges are generally non-refundable.

Payment gateway charges are generally non-refundable.

A student's decision to discontinue, postpone or not utilise a purchased programme shall not, by itself, create an entitlement to a refund.

5. EXCEPTIONAL CIRCUMSTANCES

Refund requests may be considered only in exceptional circumstances, including, where appropriate:

Duplicate payment for the same programme.

Payment made due to a demonstrable technical error.

Payment processed more than once for a single transaction.

Programme cancellation by SPEAXA without offering a reasonable alternative.

Any other exceptional circumstance determined by SPEAXA Management.

The existence of an exceptional circumstance does not automatically entitle a User to a refund. Each request shall be evaluated individually.

6. REFUND REQUEST PROCEDURE

Users seeking a refund shall:

Submit a written request through SPEAXA's official support channel.

Provide the registered name and contact details.

Provide transaction details.

State the reason for the refund request.

Submit any supporting documents reasonably requested by SPEAXA.

Cooperate during the review process.

Refund requests shall not be processed automatically through:

The Website.

Android Application.

iOS Application.

Payment Gateway.

Student Dashboard.

7. REVIEW OF REFUND REQUESTS

Every refund request shall be:

Reviewed individually.

Examined on its own facts.

Evaluated based on supporting evidence.

Assessed in accordance with SPEAXA's internal policies and applicable law.

SPEAXA may:

Approve the request.

Reject the request.

Seek additional information.

Offer an alternative remedy, such as batch transfer, course rescheduling, service credit or another suitable academic solution.

8. DECISION OF SPEAXA MANAGEMENT

The User acknowledges that:

Refund decisions involve academic, administrative and commercial considerations.

No automated approval process exists.

Every request requires internal review.

SPEAXA Management reserves the right to determine whether the circumstances justify a refund.

The decision of SPEAXA Management shall be communicated to the User and shall be final, subject to the User's rights under applicable law.

9. CANCELLATION BY THE USER

Where a User voluntarily cancels:

After enrolment.

After admission confirmation.

After batch allocation.

After commencement of classes.

Due to change of personal circumstances.

Due to relocation.

Due to schedule conflicts.

Due to lack of participation.

such cancellation shall not automatically entitle the User to any refund.

10. CANCELLATION BY SPEAXA

SPEAXA reserves the right to suspend, postpone or cancel a programme due to:

Insufficient enrolments.

Faculty unavailability.

Force majeure events.

Technical reasons.

Legal or regulatory requirements.

Operational considerations.

Where appropriate, SPEAXA may, at its discretion:

Offer a replacement batch.

Offer an alternative programme.

Offer rescheduling.

Offer service credit.

Consider a refund where no reasonable alternative is available.

11. PAYMENT FAILURES

Where a payment fails:

Registration shall not be confirmed until payment is successfully completed.

Users should contact their bank or payment provider if funds have been debited without confirmation.

SPEAXA shall reasonably cooperate in resolving genuine payment issues.

12. CHARGEBACKS

Users agree that:

Chargebacks should not be initiated without first contacting SPEAXA.

SPEAXA reserves the right to contest unjustified chargebacks.

Accounts may be temporarily restricted while chargeback disputes are under review.

13. PROMOTIONAL OFFERS

Scholarships, discounts, coupons and promotional offers:

Are subject to their respective terms.

May not be exchanged for cash.

May not be transferred unless expressly permitted.

May not create additional refund rights unless specifically stated.

14. MODE OF REFUND

If a refund is approved:

It shall normally be processed through the original payment method used for the transaction, unless otherwise permitted by law.

Applicable payment gateway charges, taxes or administrative deductions may be applied where legally permissible.

Processing timelines may vary depending on banking systems and payment service providers.

15. NO WAIVER

Consideration of a refund request in one case shall not create:

A precedent.

A continuing obligation.

An automatic entitlement for future refund requests.

Every request shall be assessed independently.

16. FRAUDULENT CLAIMS

Users shall not:

Submit false refund requests.

Provide misleading information.

Alter payment records.

Submit forged documents.

Abuse SPEAXA's refund process.

Fraudulent claims may result in:

Rejection of the request.

Suspension of the account.

Termination of services.

Appropriate legal action.

17. MODIFICATION OF THIS POLICY

SPEAXA reserves the right to:

Revise this Policy.

Introduce additional conditions.

Modify refund procedures.

Update payment processes.

Amend operational guidelines.

Updated versions shall become effective upon publication unless otherwise required by law.

18. CONTACT FOR REFUND REQUESTS

All refund or cancellation requests shall be submitted only through SPEAXA's officially notified support channels.

Users are advised not to rely on verbal assurances from teachers, counsellors or third parties regarding refund eligibility.

19. ACKNOWLEDGEMENT

By making any payment to SPEAXA, the User acknowledges that:

This Refund, Cancellation & Payment Policy has been read and understood.

Refunds are not automatic.

Refunds are considered only in exceptional circumstances.

Every request is reviewed individually.

SPEAXA may request additional documents or information before making a decision.

The User agrees to cooperate with the review process.

20. NO GUARANTEE OF ACADEMIC OUTCOMES

The User acknowledges that:

Payment to SPEAXA is for access to educational services, teaching, mentoring and learning resources.

SPEAXA does not guarantee specific examination scores, ranks, admissions, scholarships, placements or employment outcomes.

Dissatisfaction with personal academic performance or results shall not, by itself, constitute grounds for a refund.

Individual success depends on multiple factors, including attendance, participation, effort, prior preparation and consistent practice.
